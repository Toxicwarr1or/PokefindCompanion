Pokefind's PvP and most PvE battles follow **Gen 8** battle mechanics, with **no Mega Evolution** (server-wide).

## Generation rules at a glance

| Pokemon class | Movepool / TMs | Abilities |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Standard species** | Gen 6 ORAS movepool | **Gen 7** abilities |
| **Anniversary species** (Kyoto, Jataro, Haikou, Shiloh, Zeinova, etc.) | Gen 6 base + Gen 8 additions | Gen 7 base + Gen 8 additions |

This means a normal-form Pokemon plays close to ORAS-era mechanics with the Gen 7 ability list bolted on. Anniversary forms get the broader Gen 8 movepool + ability access on top of that as a deliberate power tier above standard play.

> **Mega Evolution is not supported on the server.** Anything in this section assumes no-Mega play.

## Movepool reference (Gen 6 ORAS)

Pokefind species lookups use Gen 6 ORAS movesets as the baseline — particularly relevant for tutor moves that no longer exist in later gens. The Pokedex entries already include each species' Gen 6 ORAS-era level-up moves, TMs/HMs, Move Tutor moves, and Egg Moves; check a species page for its specific movepool.

> **Authoritative source:** Bulbapedia per-generation learnset subpages (`/Generation_VI_learnset` etc.) are static HTML and the canonical reference when the wiki and the in-game data disagree.

Each species' Pokedex page lists:

- **Level-up Moves** — ORAS-era level-up table
- **TMs & HMs** — every TM/HM the species can learn in ORAS
- **Move Tutor** — ORAS tutor moves (this is the list that diverges most from later gens)
- **Egg Moves** — ORAS egg-move pool

For anniversary forms, the Pokedex tab for that form lists its own movepool, which includes Gen 8 additions on top of the Gen 6 base.
