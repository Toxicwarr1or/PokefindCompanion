---
title: "Caves and Bridges"
subtitle: "Player-specific spawn pockets that override the regional pool inside named caves and on named bridges"
---

Certain caves and bridges across the world have their own wild-spawn pool that overrides the standard regional encounter table. The trigger is **per player**, not shared — your spawns are yours alone, rolled from your steps and a short timer rather than the global region clock.

## How they work

- Walking around inside one of these zones rolls your personal spawn timer. Crossing the **150-step threshold** or standing in the zone for **two minutes** triggers a roll, whichever happens first.
- A successful spawn appears **2 to 5 blocks** from where you're standing.
- The pool replaces (not stacks with) the area's normal regional spawns while you're inside. Step out and you go back to the region's default encounter list.
- Two of the caves split their pool by terrain — a species marked **(Water)** only rolls when you're standing in water, and **(Air)** only on dry ground. The cave's wet and dry sections both feed the same overall pool.

Spawn rarities below follow the wiki's standard tiers (no per-rate percentages, by convention):

- **Common** — the species you'll see most often.
- **Uncommon** — regular but slower to encounter.
- **Rare** — uncommon visitors; expect to walk a while.
- **Ultra-Rare** — single-digit chance per roll; trophy spawns.

## Baywharf Cave

A bug-and-grass-leaning pool with a small cluster of "starter prequel" rare encounters near the bottom of the table.

- **Common:** Sewaddle, Caterpie
- **Uncommon:** Oddish, Bellsprout, Foongus, Shroomish, Petilil, Cottonee, Paras, Weedle, Cherubi, Budew
- **Rare:** Hoppip, Wurmple, Bonsly, Dwebble, Ralts, Venipede, Kricketot, Combee, Pineco, Clefairy, Jigglypuff, Sunkern, Burmy, Snubbull, Shuckle, Geodude, Venonat, Roggenrola, Tropius, Carnivine, Pinsir, Heracross
- **Ultra-Rare:** Pansage, Larvesta, Riolu

## Breezelton Bridge

Sky-themed bird pool — every encounter is a flier or a flying-adjacent species.

- **Common:** Pidgey, Pidove, Spearow, Taillow, Starly, Wingull
- **Uncommon:** Hoothoot, Ducklett, Doduo, Ledyba, Natu, Woobat
- **Rare:** Combee, Hoppip, Rufflet, Drifloon, Chatot, Zubat, Yanma, Masquerain, Murkrow, Vullaby, Mothim, Skarmory, Scyther
- **Ultra-Rare:** Dodrio, Farfetch'd

## Breezelton Cave

The split-terrain cave: the wet half draws fish and water-types, the dry half draws cave-floor staples and a couple of rare pseudo-legend prequels.

- **Common:** Magikarp (Water), Geodude (Air), Barboach (Water), Tympole (Water), Roggenrola (Air), Psyduck (Water), Sandshrew (Air), Goldeen (Water), Diglett (Air), Zubat (Air), Krabby (Water), Numel (Air)
- **Uncommon:** Onix (Air), Slowpoke (Water), Cubone (Air), Golett (Air), Wooper (Water), Corphish (Water), Drilbur (Air), Marill (Water), Frillish (Water), Dwebble (Air), Phanpy (Air), Trapinch (Air), Baltoy (Air), Buizel (Water), Audino (Air), Tentacool (Water)
- **Rare:** Nosepass (Air), Shuckle (Air), Bonsly (Air), Munna (Air), Chinchou (Water), Hippopotas (Air), Happiny (Air), Stunfisk (Water), Aron (Air), Pansage (Air), Mantyke (Water), Corsola (Water), Relicanth (Water), Wynaut (Air), Feebas (Water)
- **Ultra-Rare:** Sandile (Air), Larvitar (Air), Gible (Air)

## Northrun Bridge

Cold-weather bird-and-ice flavor — heavy on Ice-types alongside the usual flying staples, with one chance encounter at a fully-evolved Gyarados.

- **Common:** Pidgey, Pidove, Spearow, Starly
- **Uncommon:** Wingull, Ducklett, Swinub, Taillow, Hoothoot, Delibird, Cubchoo, Vanillite, Rufflet, Doduo, Mantyke, Snorunt, Snover, Spheal
- **Rare:** Natu, Woobat, Drifloon, Sneasel, Hoppip, Chatot, Cryogonal, Skarmory, Murkrow, Vullaby, Sigilyph
- **Ultra-Rare:** Dodrio, Farfetch'd, Gyarados

## Pure Harbor Bridge

Psychic-leaning sky pool — Psychic-typed birds and the Munna / Solosis / Gothita / Elgyem cluster show up here.

- **Common:** Natu, Woobat
- **Uncommon:** Pidgey, Pidove, Abra, Clefairy, Drowzee, Spearow, Taillow, Starly, Hoothoot, Jigglypuff, Sigilyph, Wingull, Ducklett, Munna, Dodrio, Marill
- **Rare:** Doduo, Snubbull, Cottonee, Spoink, Chatot, Ralts, Mantyke, Rufflet, Chingling, Solosis, Gothita, Elgyem, Mawile, Mime Jr., Skarmory
- **Ultra-Rare:** Farfetch'd, Togepi

## Redgrove Cave

Volcanic / fire-leaning cave — Slugma and Numel headline, with a steady supply of Fire-types and ground-dwellers.

- **Common:** Slugma, Numel, Geodude
- **Uncommon:** Torkoal, Vulpix, Growlithe, Roggenrola, Zubat, Darumaka, Houndour, Magby, Sandshrew, Diglett, Heatmor, Aron
- **Rare:** Cubone, Drilbur, Onix, Trapinch, Phanpy, Koffing, Litwick, Bonsly, Stunfisk, Larvesta
- **Ultra-Rare:** Pansear

## Sparkmont Bridge

Mirrors the Breezelton Bridge bird pool with one swap — Timburr replaces Ledyba in the early-game tier — so it's the bridge to head to if you want a Fighting starter alongside the standard fliers.

- **Common:** Pidgey, Pidove, Spearow, Taillow, Starly, Timburr, Wingull
- **Uncommon:** Hoothoot, Ducklett, Doduo, Ledyba, Natu, Woobat
- **Rare:** Combee, Hoppip, Rufflet, Drifloon, Chatot, Zubat, Yanma, Masquerain, Murkrow, Vullaby, Mothim, Skarmory, Scyther
- **Ultra-Rare:** Dodrio, Farfetch'd

## Voltaris Cave

The biggest split-terrain cave by far — heavily wet, with a Lotad-led lake-cave vibe and a Dratini ultra-rare at the bottom for Dragon hunters.

- **Common:** Lotad (Water), Oddish (Air), Magikarp (Water), Bellsprout (Air), Foongus (Air), Shroomish (Air), Wooper (Water), Petilil (Air), Tympole (Water), Cottonee (Air), Marill (Water), Goldeen (Water), Zubat (Air), Sewaddle (Air), Psyduck (Water), Cherubi (Air), Budew (Air)
- **Uncommon:** Buizel (Water), Surskit (Water), Krabby (Water), Corphish (Water), Snover (Air), Barboach (Water), Slowpoke (Water), Frillish (Water), Woobat (Air), Tentacool (Water), Maractus (Air)
- **Rare:** Chinchou (Water), Tangela (Air), Mantyke (Water), Tropius (Air), Carnivine (Air), Panpour (Water), Feebas (Water)
- **Ultra-Rare:** Dratini (Water)
